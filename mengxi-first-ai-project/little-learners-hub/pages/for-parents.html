The Role of Parents in Early Learning

Parents are a child's first and most important teachers. The quality of the parent-child relationship has a profound impact on a child's cognitive, social, and emotional development. According to attachment theory, a secure attachment to a caregiver provides a foundation for healthy development.

Common Parenting Myths

Myth

You need expensive toys to stimulate your child's development.

Fact

Simple, open-ended objects (cardboard boxes, blocks, art supplies) are more effective at fostering creativity and imagination.

Myth

Early academic training (reading, math) gives children a head start.

Fact

Research shows that play-based learning is more effective for long-term academic success.

Myth

Screen time is bad for young children.

Fact

High-quality, interactive screen time (in moderation) can be educational, but face-to-face interaction is more important.

Myth

Tantrums are a sign of bad parenting.

Fact

Tantrums are a normal part of development. They happen when children can't express their emotions verbally.
