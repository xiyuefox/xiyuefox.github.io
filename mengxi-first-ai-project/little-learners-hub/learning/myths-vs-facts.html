Myths vs Facts

What research actually tells us about early childhood development

The biggest myth is that more structured learning means more intelligence. The opposite is true.

Kathy Hirsh-Pasek, Einstein Never Used Flash Cards

Common Myths Debunked by Research

Myth

Playing Mozart makes babies smarter and improves math skills.

Research Says

The original study showed adults could temporarily recognize Mozart better. No evidence supports improved intelligence in babies.

Myth

Educational DVDs and apps accelerate language development.

Research Says

Screen time before age two can reduce vocabulary by approximately one thousand words per hour of viewing. Face-to-face interaction is irreplaceable.

Myth

Telling children they are smart motivates them to succeed.

Research Says

Praising intelligence creates a fixed mindset and fear of challenges. Praising effort builds resilience and love of learning.

Myth

More toys and educational materials create smarter children.

Research Says

Open-ended play with simple objects like cardboard boxes develops more creativity than expensive specialized toys.

Myth

Missing the first three years means permanent developmental damage.

Research Says

The brain remains plastic throughout life. Normal family environments provide sufficient stimulation without special interventions.

Myth

Early academic instruction gives children a head start.

Research Says

Children who start formal academics early show no advantage by third grade. Play-based learning creates deeper, lasting understanding.

Myth

Toddler tantrums are manipulation and should be ignored.

Research Says

Tantrums happen when the emotional brain overwhelms the underdeveloped rational brain. Children need co-regulation, not isolation.

Myth

Children must learn to share from an early age.

Research Says

True sharing requires theory of mind, which develops around age three to four. Forced sharing before this creates anxiety, not generosity.

Children need to feel safe before they can learn. Emotional security is the foundation of all development.

National Scientific Council on the Developing Child

迷思与事实

研究真正告诉我们关于早期儿童发展的真相

最大的迷思是更多结构化学习意味着更高智力。事实恰恰相反。

凯西·赫什-帕塞克，《爱因斯坦从不使用闪卡》

研究揭示的常见迷思

迷思

播放莫扎特音乐能让宝宝更聪明，提高数学能力。

研究表明

原始研究仅显示成年人能暂时更好地识别莫扎特的音乐。没有证据支持能提高婴儿智力。

迷思

教育类视频和应用程序能加速语言发展。

研究表明

两岁前每小时屏幕时间可能减少约一千个词汇量。面对面互动是不可替代的。

迷思

告诉孩子他们很聪明能激励他们成功。

研究表明

表扬智力会产生固定型思维模式和对挑战的恐惧。表扬努力能培养韧性和学习热情。

迷思

更多玩具和教育材料能培养更聪明的孩子。

研究表明

用简单物品如纸箱进行开放式游戏比昂贵的专业玩具更能培养创造力。

迷思

错过前三年意味着永久性发育损害。

研究表明

大脑终生保持可塑性。正常家庭环境提供的刺激已经足够，无需特别干预。

迷思

早期学术教育能让孩子赢在起跑线。

研究表明

早期开始正规学术学习的孩子到三年级时没有显示任何优势。基于游戏的学习能创造更深层、持久的理解。

迷思

幼儿发脾气是在操控大人，应该忽视。

研究表明

发脾气发生在情绪脑压倒尚未发育成熟的理性脑时。孩子需要共同调节，而非隔离。

迷思

孩子必须从小学会分享。

研究表明

真正的分享需要心智理论，这在三到四岁左右才发展。在此之前强迫分享会产生焦虑，而非慷慨。

孩子需要先感到安全才能学习。情感安全是所有发展的基础。

国家发展中儿童科学委员会
