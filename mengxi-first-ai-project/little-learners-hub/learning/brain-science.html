The Science of Early Learning

Understanding how young brains develop helps us support them better

Fifty percent is genetics, fifty percent is environment. You control half of your child's potential.

John Medina, Brain Rules for Baby

The Golden Period: Birth to Five Years

During the first five years, a child's brain creates approximately eight thousand new neural connections every second. This period of explosive growth is when the foundation for all future learning is built. The brain develops from the bottom up, with emotional regulation coming before complex reasoning.

The Five Building Blocks of Intelligence

Research identifies five key components that predict future success better than IQ tests alone.

Executive Function: The Brain's Air Traffic Control

Harvard researchers describe executive function as the brain's air traffic control system. These skills include working memory, mental flexibility, and self-control. They develop primarily through play, not instruction, and predict academic success better than early reading ability.

Play is not a break from learning. It is how young children learn.

Understanding Toddler Behavior

Toddler behavior that frustrates adults is actually healthy brain development in action.

Why do they say no to everything?
Saying no is how toddlers build a sense of self. It is identity formation, not defiance.
Why do they have tantrums?
The emotional brain develops before the rational brain. Tantrums happen when feelings overwhelm underdeveloped coping skills.
Why do they cling then push away?
This is the toddler paradox: they need independence and security simultaneously. Both needs are real.
Why are transitions so hard?
Toddlers have no concept of time. Every ending feels like permanent loss. Rituals and warnings help bridge the gap.

The Environment of Relationships

The National Scientific Council on the Developing Child emphasizes that young children develop in the context of relationships. Serve and return interactions, where adults respond to children's cues, build brain architecture more effectively than any educational product.
