Age Stages of Development

Understanding developmental milestones and how to support them

Development is not a race. It's a journey that unfolds at its own pace.

0-12 Months: Sensorimotor Stage

According to Piaget's theory of cognitive development, the sensorimotor stage is the first stage of cognitive development, lasting from birth to approximately 2 years. During this stage, infants use their senses and motor actions to explore and understand the world.

0-6 months
Early Sensorimotor
  • Tracks moving objects with eyes
  • Recognizes familiar faces and voices
  • Reaches for and grasps objects
  • Babbles with vowels

6-12 months
Late Sensorimotor
  • Sits independently
  • Transfers objects from hand to hand
  • Starts crawling or walking
  • Says first words (mama, dada)

12-24 Months: Preoperational Stage (Early Phase)

During the early preoperational stage, toddlers begin to use symbols to represent objects. They are egocentric, meaning they have difficulty seeing things from another person's perspective.

  • Uses 5-10 words
  • Follows simple commands
  • Shows affection (hugs, kisses)

Late Toddler
  • Uses 2-word phrases
  • Points to body parts
  • Engages in parallel play
  • Shows defiance (says "no")

24-36 Months: Preoperational Stage (Middle Phase)

Toddlers become more symbolic and imaginative during this stage. They begin to engage in pretend play and develop a better understanding of language.

3-5 Years: Preoperational Stage (Late Phase)

Preschoolers become more logical and less egocentric. They develop better language skills and begin to understand basic concepts like numbers, letters, and shapes.
