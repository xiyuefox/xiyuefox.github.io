📍 Birth - 2 months

The First "Conversations"

Emma is 6 weeks old. Sarah is learning to read her cues.

👶
Waaah! Waaah!
(Crying)
"Oh sweetie, what do you need? Are you hungry?"
(Picks up Emma, speaks softly)
👩
👶
Ahh... ahh...
(Stops crying, focuses on mom's face)
"There you go. Mama's here. Let's get you fed."
(Makes eye contact, smiles)
👩
🔬

What's Happening Here

Serve and Return: Even at 6 weeks, this is a real conversation! Emma "serves" by crying, Sarah "returns" by responding. This back-and-forth builds neural connections.

  • ✓ Emma learns: "When I signal, someone responds"
  • ✓ Trust and attachment are forming
  • ✓ Sarah's tone and facial expressions matter more than words
100%
of language learning happens through responsive interaction
📍 3 months

The Social Smile

Emma now coos and smiles in response to interaction.

"Good morning, sunshine! How's my happy girl?"
(Leans close, animated voice)
👩
👶
😊 Oooh! Aaahh!
(Big smile, kicking legs)
"Ooooh! You're talking to Mama! Yes you are!"
(Mirrors Emma's sounds)
👩
👶
Goo goo! Ahh-goo!
(Cooing, waving arms)
"Ahh-goo! I hear you, sweet pea!"
(Takes turns, waits for responses)
👩
💡

Milestone Unlocked: Turn-Taking

Proto-conversations: This cooing exchange teaches the rhythm of conversation—speak, listen, respond, repeat.

  • ✓ Emma learns the pattern of dialogue
  • ✓ Mirroring sounds validates Emma's communication
  • ✓ Each "turn" strengthens vocal development
8,000
new brain connections per second at this age
📍 6-9 months

Babbling and Joint Attention

Emma babbles constantly and points to objects.

👶
Ba-ba-ba! Da-da!
(Points at toy on shelf)
"You want the ball? The red ball up there?"
(Follows Emma's gaze, points too)
👩
👶
Ba! Ba!
(Gets excited, reaches)
"Here's the BALL! Ball for Emma. Baaallll."
(Hands toy, emphasizes word)
👩
👶
Baaah! 😄
(Hugs ball, looks at mom)
🎯

The Magic of Joint Attention

Triadic Communication: Emma, Sarah, and the ball create a three-way interaction. This is when word-learning accelerates!

  • ✓ Pointing = "Let's focus on this together"
  • ✓ Sarah names what Emma's interested in (following the child's lead)
  • ✓ Repetition + context = word learning
3-5x
more words learned when adults follow baby's focus
📍 12 months

The First Real Words!

Emma's babbling transforms into recognizable words.

👶
Mama!
(Reaches arms up)
"You said MAMA! Yes! I'm Mama! Up you go!"
(Picks Emma up, eyes welling up)
👩
👶
Buh! (points)
(Wanting her book)
"Book? You want your book? Let's read!"
(Expanding on Emma's word)
👩
👶
Buh! Buh!
(Excited, patting the book)
🎉

Words Are Tools

Intentional Communication: Emma now uses specific sounds to make specific things happen. This is the birth of true language!

  • ✓ "Mama" gets attention and comfort
  • ✓ "Buh" (book) initiates a desired activity
  • ✓ Sarah "expands" - modeling the full, correct word
10-20
words in typical vocabulary by first birthday
📍 18-24 months

The Language Explosion

Emma learns ~1 new word every 2 hours she's awake.

👧
Doggy outside! Big doggy!
(Pulls mom to window)
"Yes! That's a big brown dog! I wonder where he's going?"
(Adding descriptive words)
👩
👧
Walk! Doggy walk!
(Two-word combinations!)
"Right! The dog is going for a walk with his person. Maybe we can take a walk too?"
(Expanding to full sentences)
👩
👧
Yes! Walk now! Shoes!
(Runs to get shoes)
"You want to put on your shoes and go for a walk! Great idea!"
(Models complete thought)
👩
🚀

The Rocket Ship Phase

Explosive Growth: Between 18-24 months, vocabulary can grow from 50 to 300+ words. Grammar emerges spontaneously.

  • ✓ Two-word combinations show understanding of grammar
  • ✓ Emma initiates topics (shared attention from her interests)
  • ✓ Sarah provides "scaffolding" - expanding Emma's telegraphic speech
200-300
words by age 2 (typical development)

Every Conversation Matters

From Emma's first cry to her first sentences, Sarah responded to every "serve." This is how babies learn language—not from screens, but from real, responsive, face-to-face conversations.

🌟 Key Takeaways

🎾

Serve & Return

Every baby sound deserves a response. This builds neural connections.

👀

Follow Their Lead

Talk about what interests the baby. They learn faster this way.

📚

Expand & Model

Turn "buh" into "Yes, that's your book!" Show the full language.

⏰

Quantity Matters

30 million word gap by age 3. Talk constantly!
