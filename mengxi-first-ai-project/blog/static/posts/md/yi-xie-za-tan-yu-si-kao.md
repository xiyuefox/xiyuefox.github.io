## O'Reilly Is NOT the Right Platform for Early Childhood Education

O'Reilly Media specializes in **technology books** (programming, IT, design). They have very few books about teaching young children.

---

## What I Found in Your Playlist That's Useful

|Book|How it helps YOU|
|---|---|
|**Make: Math Teacher's Supplement**|Teaching strategies for math concepts|
|**Math 100 Ideas in 100 Words**|Simple math concepts explained visually|

That's it. The rest (Python, Arduino, etc.) is for older kids.

## Better Resources for Ages 0-5

### Free Resources (No Cost)

|Resource|What it is|Link|
|---|---|---|
|**ZERO TO THREE**|Research-based infant/toddler development|zerotothree.org|
|**NAEYC**|Early childhood education best practices|naeyc.org|
|**PBS Parents**|Play-based activity ideas|pbs.org/parents|
|**Stanford DREME**|Preschool math education|prek-math-te.stanford.edu|

### Books to Buy/Borrow (Highly Recommended)

|Book|Author|Why it's essential|
|---|---|---|
|**Brain Rules for Baby**|John Medina|Science of baby brain development, 0-5|
|**Einstein Never Used Flashcards**|Hirsh-Pasek & Golinkoff|Why play beats worksheets|
|**The Whole-Brain Child**|Siegel & Bryson|How young brains develop|
|**Mind in the Making**|Ellen Galinsky|7 essential life skills for children|
|**The Scientist in the Crib**|Gopnik, Meltzoff, Kuhl|How babies think and learn|
|**The Importance of Being Little**|Erika Christakis|What preschoolers really need|
|**How Toddlers Thrive**|Tovah Klein|Understanding ages 2-5|

## My Recommendation

### For Your Website Content

Read these **3 books first** (most practical for your project):

1. **Brain Rules for Baby** - Science-backed activities by age
2. **Einstein Never Used Flashcards** - Play-based learning research
3. **Mind in the Making** - Specific skills to develop

### For Teaching Math (Ages 0-5)

Use **Stanford DREME** (free) - it's designed exactly for what you want to build!
