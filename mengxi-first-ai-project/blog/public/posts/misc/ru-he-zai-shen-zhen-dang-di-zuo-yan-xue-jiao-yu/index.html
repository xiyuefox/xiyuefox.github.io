Simulation Activity - Artificial Intelligence Drama Workshop

Grade 3 - Artificial Intelligence Drama Exploration

Learning Objectives:

  • Identify and describe examples of artificial intelligence in everyday life, such as voice assistants or smart devices.
  • Demonstrate understanding of the concept of artificial intelligence by participating in a group discussion or role play activity.
  • Collaborate with peers to create a short skit or play that incorporates artificial intelligence characters or themes.
  • Analyze and discuss the potential benefits and challenges of artificial intelligence in society, using age-appropriate language and examples.
  • Reflect on personal opinions and feelings about artificial intelligence through a written or artistic response, such as a drawing or short paragraph.

Simulation Activity:

Organize the students into small groups and assign each group a specific scenario related to artificial intelligence. Provide them with some background information and resources to understand the topic. Each group will then create a short skit or play that incorporates artificial intelligence characters or themes.

Steps:

  1. Introduce the concept of artificial intelligence to the students and provide examples of AI in everyday life.
  2. Divide the students into small groups and assign each group a specific scenario related to artificial intelligence. Examples of scenarios could include:
    • A voice assistant helping a family organize their daily activities.
    • A smart device controlling the temperature and lighting in a house.
    • An AI-powered robot assisting with household chores.
  3. Provide the groups with some resources, such as articles, videos, or images, to help them understand and research their assigned scenario.
  4. Ask each group to brainstorm and create a short skit or play that incorporates artificial intelligence characters or themes.
  5. Give the groups time to rehearse and prepare their skits or plays.
  6. Allow each group to perform their skit or play in front of the class.
  7. After each performance, facilitate a group discussion to analyze and discuss the potential benefits and challenges of artificial intelligence in the scenario portrayed.
  8. Encourage students to reflect on their personal opinions and feelings about artificial intelligence by having them write a short paragraph or create a drawing that represents their thoughts.
  9. Provide an opportunity for students to share their reflections and discuss their artistic responses with the class.

Assessment:

Assess student performance in the experiential learning activity based on the following criteria:

  • Understanding and description of artificial intelligence in their skit or play.
  • Active participation and collaboration within the group.
  • Analytical and thoughtful contributions to the group discussion.
  • Reflection and expression of personal opinions and feelings in their written or artistic response.

Age Group:

The AI drama workshop is suitable for Grade 3 students.

Resources and Support:

To successfully conduct the AI drama workshop, the following resources and support are needed:

  • Access to examples and resources on artificial intelligence.
  • Materials for creating skits or plays, such as costumes or props.
  • Space for group rehearsals and performances.
  • Facilitation and guidance from the educator to support student learning and engagement.

AI Applications in Drama and Performing Arts

Artificial intelligence (AI) is increasingly being integrated into drama and performing arts, enhancing various aspects of production, performance, and audience engagement. In the paper “Analysis of the Stage Performance Effect of Environmental Protection Music and Dance Drama Based on Artificial Intelligence Technology,” AI is used to analyze and improve stage performance by evaluating factors like audience-actor distance, actor training, and musical accompaniment, emphasizing the importance of these elements in environmental-themed performances (Zeng, 2022). The study “Technology Based on Interactive Theatre Performance Production and Performance Platform” discusses the use of AI, specifically face recognition technology, to enhance interactive theatre experiences, allowing for more personalized and engaging performances (Xia & Tian, 2022). “Machine Performers: Agents in a Multiple Ontological State” explores the embodiment of robotic performers on stage, merging AI with performing arts to redefine the concept of “embodiment” and enhance audience engagement through advanced robotic performances (Demers, 2015). In “Creation of Drama Art Based on Deep Learning and Evolution Strategy,” deep learning algorithms are used to inspire and create new forms of drama, highlighting the potential of AI to generate creative content and engage new audiences (Tang, 2022). Lastly, “Performatology: A Procedural Acting Approach for Interactive Drama in Cinematic Games” combines performing arts theory with AI to design Performative Embodied Agents (PEAs), simulating skilled acting in interactive dramas and enhancing the believability and appeal of non-player characters (NPCs) in cinematic games (Maraffi & Jhala, 2011).
